Intel Sustainability Milestones

1968 • Intel Founded

Noyce & Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation.

This lays the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971 • First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004—the first commercial microprocessor.

It kickstarts the modern computing era.

1978 • 8086 Processor

Launch of 8086 establishes the x86 architecture.

x86 powers PCs & servers worldwide.

1985 • 386 Processor

First 32-bit x86 CPU boosts performance & multitasking.

A major step for personal computing.

2006 • Peak GHG Emissions

Highest annual operational emissions.

Followed by major abatement, renewables, and efficiency work.

2020 • RISE Strategy

Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling (2030 goals).

Focus on climate action, water, and waste.

2022 • Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to net-zero (Scope 1 & 2) across operations.

Built on long-running environmental initiatives.

2023 • Renewable Electricity

99% renewable electricity worldwide.

Helps drive carbon reductions.

2024 • Sustainability Summit

Intel convenes partners and leaders.

Collaboration on sustainable manufacturing.
